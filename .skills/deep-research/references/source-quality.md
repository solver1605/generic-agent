# Source Quality and Evidence Policy

## Reliability Tiers

Tier 1 (preferred):
- Official docs, standards bodies, primary datasets, peer-reviewed papers, regulator/government publications.

Tier 2 (acceptable with caution):
- Reputable industry analysis, major technical publications, established vendor technical blogs.

Tier 3 (supporting only):
- Community posts, forums, unsourced opinion articles, marketing pages without verifiable evidence.

## Citation Rules

- Attach at least one citation for each non-trivial claim.
- For high-impact claims, include at least two independent sources.
- Include URL and publication/update date whenever available.

## Recency Rules

- Use explicit recency filtering for time-sensitive topics.
- Flag stale evidence when publication date is old relative to claim sensitivity.

## Contradiction Handling

- Do not hide conflicting evidence.
- Present conflict, evaluate source quality, and state confidence boundaries.

## Uncertainty Policy

- Separate facts, inferences, and assumptions.
- Explicitly call out unknowns and what would reduce uncertainty.
